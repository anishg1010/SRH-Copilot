# CORE Competence Framework — Sample (replace with your institution's real documents)

## Purpose
The CORE framework guides competence-oriented teaching. Every module defines what
students can *do* after completion, not merely what they were exposed to.

## Competence Dimensions
- **Knowledge & Understanding** — foundational concepts and their relationships.
- **Application & Analysis** — using knowledge in realistic, discipline-specific problems.
- **Synthesis & Creation** — producing original work: designs, arguments, artifacts.
- **Reflection & Responsibility** — evaluating one's own work, ethics, impact,
  including the responsible use of AI tools.

## Writing Learning Objectives
Objectives must be observable and measurable. Use action verbs aligned to the intended
cognitive level (identify, apply, analyze, design, evaluate). Avoid *understand, know,
learn about*. Each module should span multiple cognitive levels rather than clustering at recall.

## Module Descriptor Template
Every module descriptor contains: title, ECTS credits, level, 4–8 learning objectives,
session-by-session plan, teaching/learning methods, assessment format, and rubric.
Constructive alignment across objectives, methods, and assessment is mandatory.

## Responsible AI in Teaching
Lecturers may integrate AI as a learning aid where it develops competence (e.g. students
critiquing AI output, iterative drafting with reflection). AI must not replace the
demonstration of the student's own competence in graded assessment.

## Assessment & Rubrics
Assessments must validly measure the stated objectives. Analytic rubrics define 3–5
criteria across performance levels: Insufficient, Sufficient, Good, Excellent. Criteria
must be observable and traceable to specific learning objectives.
